Pet Insurance for Older Dogs and Cats

Insuring a senior pet is possible — but the rules and pricing differ. Here's what to know before you buy.

Age limits vary by insurer

Some providers cap enrollment age; others have no upper age limit. If your pet is older, prioritize those without caps.

Expect higher premiums

Senior pets cost more to insure, and pre-existing conditions are excluded — so the earlier you enroll, the better the value.

What to prioritize

For older pets, a solid annual limit and illness coverage matter more than wellness extras.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I get pet insurance for a 10-year-old dog?

Yes, with providers that have no upper age limit — though pre-existing conditions won't be covered.

Is senior pet insurance worth it?

It can be, since older pets need care more often; compare annual limits carefully.

Note on pricing: Cost ranges on this page are illustrative 2026 market averages for accident-and-illness coverage. Your actual quote depends on your pet's breed, age, and ZIP code. Always confirm current prices and coverage directly with each provider.
