Lemonade vs Spot Pet Insurance (2026)

Lemonade wins on price and a fast, app-first claims experience; Spot wins on broader base coverage, no age limit, and unlimited annual payouts. Both reimburse up to 90% with annual deductibles — so the right pick comes down to your pet's age and how much coverage you want built in versus added on.

Quick verdict: Choose Lemonade if you want the lowest price and a slick app for a young, healthy pet. Choose Spot if your pet is older, or you want dental illness, behavioral therapy, and exam fees in the base plan with an unlimited annual cap.

Cost: Lemonade is cheaper

At the base-plan level, Lemonade consistently costs less — often $5–$15 per month less than Spot for the same pet profile. Sample gaps reported for 2026: a puppy around $19/mo with Lemonade vs $25 with Spot, and a senior cat around $32 vs $72. But part of that gap is because Spot builds in coverage (dental illness, behavioral therapy, exam fees) that Lemonade sells as paid riders — so compare like-for-like once you add the extras you want.

Coverage: Spot includes more by default

Lemonade uses an open-peril structure (everything is covered unless specifically excluded) and keeps the base plan lean, with wellness and vet-fee coverage as add-ons. Spot bakes dental illness, behavioral therapy, and vet exam fees into its base plan and offers an unlimited annual limit, versus Lemonade's $100,000 cap. For most pets $100k is plenty; for breeds prone to very costly chronic conditions, Spot's unlimited option is reassuring.

Age & waiting periods

This is where they split most clearly. Spot has no upper age limit, so you can enroll a senior pet; Lemonade won't start new coverage for pets 14 or older in most states. On accidents, Lemonade has no waiting period in most states, while Spot requires 14 days (next-day accident coverage in select states). Both still apply illness and longer orthopedic waiting periods.

Which should you choose?

  • Pick Lemonade if: you want the lowest premium, an app-first fast-claims experience, and you're insuring a young or middle-aged pet.
  • Pick Spot if: your pet is older (or you plan to keep it for life), you want dental/behavioral/exam fees included without riders, you want an unlimited annual cap, or you prioritize customer-satisfaction ratings.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Lemonade or Spot cheaper?

Lemonade is usually cheaper — roughly $5–$15/mo less than Spot for the same pet and settings. But Spot's higher base premium includes dental illness, behavioral therapy, and vet exam fees that Lemonade charges extra for.

Which is better for older pets?

Spot — it has no upper age limit, so senior pets can enroll. Lemonade won't start new coverage for pets aged 14 or older in most states.

Does Lemonade or Spot have a shorter waiting period?

Lemonade has no accident waiting period in most states; Spot's is 14 days (next-day in some states). Illness and orthopedic waits still apply to both.

Which has a higher annual limit?

Spot offers an unlimited annual limit option; Lemonade caps its maximum at $100,000.

Is Spot or Lemonade better overall?

Neither wins outright. Lemonade is best for budget, fast app claims, and younger pets; Spot is best for older pets, broader base coverage, unlimited limits, and higher ratings.
